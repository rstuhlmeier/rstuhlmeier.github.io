Water Waves - Mathematical Theory and Applications

A workshop to be held at the University of Plymouth, September 5-6, 2019, on theoretical and applied aspects of water waves.

If you are interested in attending, please register using this link or email the organizer Raphael Stuhlmeier.

Speakers

  • David Andrade (Technion) - tbd
  • Ton van den Bremer (Oxford) - Dispersion and Modulational Instability in Crossing Waves
  • Anna Kalogirou (Nottingham) - Wave amplification phenomena and a novel wave-energy converter
  • Dan Liberzon (Technion) - Breaking of water waves - criterion and accurate detection
  • Kateryna Marynets (Vienna) - On a Two-Point Boundary Value Problem in Geophysics
  • Emilian Parau (UEA) - Water waves under ice and related phenomena
  • Alison Raby (Plymouth) - Characteristics of design waves in shallow water
  • Raphael Stuhlmeier (Plymouth) - Exact solutions for deep-water waves - Gerstner's wave and recent extensions

Funded by a Celebrating New Appointments Grant from the London Mathematical Society.
