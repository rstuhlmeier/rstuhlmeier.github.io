\begin{enumerate}
\item[*1.] Find the regions in the $xy$ plane where the equation 
\[ y u_{xx} - 2 u_{xy} + x u_{yy} = 0 \]
is elliptic, hyperbolic, or parabolic. % Strauss p. 31, Example 2.
\item[2.] Reduce the elliptic equation
\[ u_{xx} + 3u_{yy} - 2u_x + 24 u_y + 5 u = 0 \]
to the form $v_{xx} + v_{yy} + cv = 0$ by a change of dependent variable $u = ve^{\alpha x + \beta y}$ and then a change of scale $y' = \gamma y.$ %Strauss p. 32 Ex. 5
\item[*3.] Consider the equation 
\[ u_{xx} + y u_{yy} = 0.\]
Find the canonical form of the equation for the domain where it is hyperbolic. %Tricomi equation, P&R Question 3.12
\item[4.] Consider the equation 
\[ xu_{xx} - yu_{yy} + \frac{1}{2}(u_x - u_y) = 0. \]
Find the domain where the equation is elliptic, and the domain where it is hyperbolic. For each of the two domains, find the corresponding canonical transformation. % P&R Question 3.5
\item[**5.] (Optional) Reduce the following second-order equation to a system of first-order equations.
\[ u_{tt} - 4u_{xt} - 5u_{xx} = 0 \]
Use the method of characteristics to find the general solution. %Stanford HW 4
\end{enumerate}
